## The problem

Ledgerline's finance customers ran month-end close against a normalized OLTP
schema. Every report was a fan-out of joins across seven tables, and the
biggest tenants were waiting **40 minutes** for a P&L rollup.

## The approach

We rebuilt the reporting path as an event-sourced ledger:

- Every mutation appends an immutable ledger event.
- A projector folds events into per-tenant reporting tables, denormalized for
  the exact queries the dashboard runs.
- Projections rebuild from scratch in under ten minutes, so schema changes
  stopped being migrations and became replays.

> The best part of an append-only ledger is that "what did this number look
> like last Tuesday?" stops being an engineering ticket.

## Results

| Metric | Before | After |
| --- | --- | --- |
| P&L rollup (2M txns) | 40 min | 0.8 s |
| Storage cost | baseline | +18% |
| Audit questions per close | ~12 | 0 |

The dashboard now streams results as projections update, and the audit team
reads the ledger directly.
